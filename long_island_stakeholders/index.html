01 / Executive Summary

Mapping the Institutional Landscape

This memo maps the institutional landscape supporting Long Island's manufacturing ecosystem, identifies the datasets held by different actors, and recommends priority stakeholders for Phase 2 outreach.

The central finding is that Long Island has a structurally thinner institutional support infrastructure than every comparable peer region — no dedicated manufacturing innovation center, no state-level manufacturing fund, and an MEP center operating on roughly one-tenth the budget of Connecticut's CCAT.

The $30 million ACHIEVE award for a Regional Commercialization Corridor — to be operated by Newlab — represents a generational opportunity to close these gaps.

Engaging the right stakeholders now will shape both the data available for Phase 2 company-level research and the partnerships necessary for Phase 3 validation.

02 / The Ecosystem Architecture

Five Functional Layers

Long Island's manufacturing support infrastructure is organized across five functional layers. Unlike peer regions where these functions are often coordinated through a single anchor institution or state-level manufacturing office, Long Island's ecosystem is polycentric and loosely networked — multiple organizations serve overlapping constituencies without a clear hierarchy.

Key Structural Observation

The layers are weakly connected vertically. HIA-LI and Ignite LI convene firms but have limited connections to BNL's research capabilities. LIMEP links Stony Brook to manufacturers but has a $1.27M annual budget — insufficient for the scale of the ecosystem. The ACHIEVE award / Newlab center is positioned to bridge the convening and knowledge layers for the first time.

03 / Academic & Research Institutions

Knowledge Anchors

These institutions anchor the knowledge and technology dimensions of the ecosystem. Their missions range from fundamental research (BNL) to applied commercialization (Stony Brook's SPIR/AERTC) to workforce training (SCCC, Farmingdale).

Stony Brook University

Institutional anchor with $4.65B annual regional economic impact. Hosts LIMEP (MEP center), AERTC (clean energy), CEBIP (cleantech incubator), CEWIT (wireless/IT), SPIR (industry partnerships — 3,100+ projects with 490+ firms), LIHTI (incubator, 55% of graduates still in business), SBDC, and the Calverton food incubator. The largest single-site employer on Long Island.

Brookhaven National Laboratory

DOE national laboratory. NSLS-II synchrotron (29 beamlines) and Center for Functional Nanomaterials enabling materials characterization and manufacturing R&D. Technologist-in-Residence partnerships with Northrop Grumman and Henkel. Conceptual Discovery Park (~60 acres) for tech transfer.

Farmingdale State College

ABET-accredited B.S. in Manufacturing Engineering Technology. Broad Hollow Bioscience Park (102,500 sq ft, hosts Estee Lauder R&D). Co-leads NY Offshore Wind Training Institute with Stony Brook.

Composite Prototyping Center

Only composite design, prototyping, and automated fiber placement facility in the northeastern United States. Located in Plainview.

04 / Industry Associations

Connective Tissue

These organizations represent manufacturers collectively, facilitate networking, and coordinate supply chain relationships. They are the primary connective tissue of the ecosystem.

HIA-LI

Steward of the Long Island Innovation Park at Hauppauge — 1,400+ companies, 55,000+ employees, $13B annual output. Runs the region's largest B2B trade show (4,000+ attendees). The central convening node for the park ecosystem.

Ignite Long Island

501(c)(6) trade association representing all 12 manufacturing sectors in Nassau and Suffolk. Operates the Connex New York Marketplace (supply chain matching tool), the Industry/Education Portal, and the Connector Series events. NYS Apprenticeship Alliance Partner.

ADDAPT

Aerospace Defense Diversification Alliance for Productivity and Training. Coordinates the A&D supply chain, advocates on Capitol Hill and at the Pentagon. Based at the Cradle of Aviation Museum.

Long Island Association

Largest regional business association. Manufacturing & Trade Committee examines manufacturing across biotech, pharma, aerospace, electronics, and food/agriculture. Workforce Development Roundtable (2025-2026, funded by JPMorgan Chase/NY Community Trust).

Triangulation strategy: No single source covers all dimensions. Our Phase 2 approach will start with USASpending recipient names (firm names + NAICS + contracts), cross-reference with SAM.gov (certifications), augment with ThomasNet (capabilities + equipment), and validate through LIMEP/Ignite LI partnership (ground truth). Public sources give us the skeleton; partner data fills in capabilities.

Key Data Gap

No organization holds a unified database of manufacturer capabilities, certifications, and capacity. The most promising strategy for Phase 2 is to triangulate across multiple sources: start with USASpending recipient names (A&D firms), cross-reference with SAM.gov (certifications), augment with ThomasNet (capabilities), and validate through LIMEP/HIA-LI partnership (ground truth).
