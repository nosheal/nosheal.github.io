01 — Overview

The Battery Manufacturing Ecosystem in New York

This report maps 267 organizations involved in battery and energy storage manufacturing across New York State: 103 manufacturers, 40 institutional support organizations, and 124 service providers.

New York has globally recognized battery research (two DOE Energy Frontier Research Centers, a Nobel laureate), the only dedicated state-level battery consortium in the US (NY-BEST), and the largest state storage deployment mandate (6 GW by 2030). The central question this report examines: how effectively does the institutional support architecture connect these assets to the 107 manufacturers who could produce batteries in New York?

The state's identified manufacturing-side incentives (~$200–300M) are roughly 20–30x smaller than its deployment commitments (~$5–6B). Of 12 manufacturers with documented institutional connections, none received support across more than 3 of 6 institutional layers. 95 of 107 manufacturers have no documented institutional connections at all.

06 — Observations

Patterns in the Data

Deployment vs. Manufacturing Investment

Identified state deployment commitments total $5–6B (Bulk ISC, retail/residential incentives, NY Green Bank). Identified state manufacturing commitments total ~$200–300M (Excelsior credits, REDC awards, NYSERDA innovation, NY-BEST seed). Excelsior deal sizes for battery companies range from $250K (Natrion) to $13.5M (Li-Cycle, never triggered).

Pre-Revenue Capital

iM3NY (total debt: $136M) and Li-Cycle (construction costs: ~$960M vs. $175M estimate) both entered bankruptcy during the gap between project financing and operational revenue. Performance-based Excelsior credits, triggered by job creation milestones, do not flow during the pre-operational period. Li-Cycle's $475M DOE ATVM loan required $265M in independent funding before first disbursement.

Geographic Distribution of Support

82 of 124 service providers (66%) are in New York City. The three upstate regions with the most manufacturers — Southern Tier (24), Finger Lakes (20), Western NY (20) — share approximately 20 local support organizations. None of New York's 11 MEP centers has documented battery-specific programming.

Workforce

Battery-specific workforce programming (NENY Battery Academy, Broome-Tioga BOCES New Energy Lab) dates to 2023 or later. No battery-manufacturing-specific registered apprenticeship exists in New York. No SUNY community college offers a battery manufacturing degree or certificate.

07 — What If

Where Coordination Could Add Value

The journey data in Section 05 reveals that no company in this dataset received support across more than 3 of 6 institutional layers. Across the 12 manufacturers with documented connections, each assembled its own support independently. These observations frame where a coordinating function could change outcomes.

Sionic Energy spun out of Cornell in 2011. Its first documented capital connection (a $100K NSF SuperBoost grant) arrived in 2025 — 14 years later. Conamix (2014 spinout) has no documented capital connection at all. Meanwhile, ESD deployed $12.75M in Excelsior credits to 3 battery companies in 2022–2024 (Electrovaya $4M, BAE Systems $8.5M, Natrion $250K). The question: could the existing capital programs reach spinouts earlier if someone brokered the connection? The NENY ChargeUp Accelerator (launched 2022) now serves this function for companies entering the Southern Tier ecosystem, but it did not exist during the founding years of these four companies.

Anovion manufactures synthetic graphite anode material at Sanborn, NY — a supply chain segment where Chinese suppliers hold an estimated 97% global share. When its $117M DOE grant was cancelled, there was no state-level support to fall back on. The $800M production facility is now planned for Bainbridge, Georgia. The R&D hub remains in NY. Graphenix, separately, raised €20M from the European Investment Bank — roughly 7x its total identified NY support (~$3.1M). Its first commercial factory is being built in Germany. In both cases, the companies found capital elsewhere. The question is whether state engagement earlier in the process would have changed which geography captured the production.
